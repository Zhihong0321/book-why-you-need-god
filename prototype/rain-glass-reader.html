I. Stillness Before the Storm

There is a moment — just before the rain begins — when the world holds its breath. The leaves stop rustling. The birds go quiet. And in that silence, something ancient stirs.

You've felt it before. That pause between one thought and the next, where everything you thought you knew dissolves into mist.

II. The Weight of Water

The rain comes now. Not gently — but with purpose. Each drop carries the weight of a sky that could no longer hold itself together. It falls because it must.

And isn't that how truth arrives? Not when we're ready, but when the pressure becomes too great for silence to contain it.

III. Downpour

The glass shakes. The world outside becomes a blur of green and grey. You can barely see the trees now — just shapes, moving like memories half-forgotten.

This is where most people look away. The intensity is too much. But stay. Stay behind the glass. You are safe here. Watch what the storm reveals.

IV. Patterns in the Glass

The water traces paths down the window. No two streams are alike, yet they all find their way down. Gravity doesn't negotiate. It simply is.

Some truths are like that. They don't argue. They don't persuade. They simply exist, waiting for you to stop resisting them.

V. After

The rain softens. The forest breathes again. Light finds its way through — not the harsh light of certainty, but the gentle glow of dawn after a long night.

You're still here. Behind the glass. But something has shifted. The question is no longer whether the storm was real. The question is what you'll do now that you've seen it.
